AI Receptionist — Client Fulfillment Runbook

Step-by-step process from first inquiry to live AI agent for a paying client.
Price: +$249/mo recurring Setup time: 3-5 hours total Go-live target: Same day or next day Dependencies: Existing CRM + Google Calendar

What You Are Setting Up

A Vapi.ai voice agent connected to the client's forwarded phone number. When someone calls, the AI answers in under 2 seconds, handles FAQs, books appointments to the client's Google Calendar, and logs every caller as a lead in their CRM automatically. You are the builder and operator. The client just forwards their number and logs into the CRM they already have.

What you need from the client

  • Business name and phone number to forward
  • Services list with pricing (or ranges)
  • Hours of operation
  • Booking link or calendar access (already set up for CRM clients)
  • Any FAQs they get asked constantly
  • Preferred AI voice tone (professional, friendly, etc.)

What you will set up

  • Vapi.ai account and assistant configuration
  • Phone number via Twilio (~$1/mo)
  • System prompt generated from client info
  • Google Calendar booking action in Vapi
  • Webhook to client's Supabase leads table
  • End-to-end call test before go-live

Phase 1: Qualification and Intake

When an inquiry comes in asking about the AI Receptionist add-on, do this before any technical work.

1
Qualify the inquiry on a quick call (15 min)
15 min
  • Confirm they are an existing web client or want to bundle with a site tier
  • Ask: how many inbound calls do you miss per week? If the answer is zero, the value prop is weak. This is for businesses who are on the job and genuinely miss calls.
  • Ask: do you use Google Calendar for scheduling? If no, ask what they use. Vapi can also send a booking link via SMS instead of direct calendar booking.
  • Confirm they understand this is an AI, not a human. Some clients have concerns. Framing: "It answers like a knowledgeable assistant who knows your full service menu and your schedule. Most callers don't notice."
  • Confirm pricing: $249/mo recurring, billed same day as their other services
2
Collect intake information (async — send a short form or email)
Client fills out
  • Business name and primary phone number (the one customers call)
  • Full services list with prices or price ranges
  • Hours: days open, hours open, holiday policy
  • Top 5 questions callers ask most often, with the correct answers
  • What should happen if the AI cannot answer something? Options: "Tell caller you will call back within X hours" or "Transfer to your cell number"
  • Voice preference: professional and formal, or friendly and casual?
  • Preferred agent name (default: "Hi, thanks for calling [Business Name]" — no fake name needed)
3
Collect first payment before building
Before any setup
  • Send QuickBooks invoice for first month ($249)
  • Add to their recurring invoice going forward
  • Do not begin Phase 2 until payment clears

Phase 2: Infrastructure Setup

4
Create Vapi.ai assistant
45-60 min
One Vapi account, multiple assistants. You manage one Vapi account for all clients. Each client gets their own assistant configuration. Usage is billed to your Vapi account and rolled into the $249 charge.
  • Log into vapi.ai, create a new Assistant
  • Set the model to GPT-4o or Claude (either works; GPT-4o has lower latency for voice)
  • Choose a voice — Vapi has 50+ options. Pick one that matches the tone the client requested.
  • Write the system prompt (see template below)
  • Set max call duration (recommend 8 minutes — prevents runaway calls)
  • Enable call transcription (Deepgram, included in Vapi)
System Prompt Template — fill in [BRACKETS]
You are the AI phone receptionist for [BUSINESS NAME], a [INDUSTRY TYPE] service business in [CITY, STATE]. Your job is to answer calls professionally, answer questions about services and pricing, and book appointments. BUSINESS HOURS: [DAYS AND HOURS] SERVICES AND PRICING: [LIST EACH SERVICE AND PRICE/RANGE] FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS: [LIST EACH Q AND A] BOOKING: When a caller wants to schedule, ask for their name, phone number, and preferred date and time. Then use the calendar tool to check availability and confirm the booking. If no slots are available, offer the next available time. WHEN YOU CANNOT ANSWER: If you do not know the answer to something, say: "That is a great question. Let me have [OWNER NAME] follow up with you directly. Can I get your name and the best number to reach you?" Then log the callback request. TONE: [PROFESSIONAL AND DIRECT / FRIENDLY AND CONVERSATIONAL — pick one] Do not mention that you are an AI unless directly asked. If asked, confirm honestly that you are an AI assistant. Do not make up pricing. If uncertain, give the range and say the owner will confirm on the job.
5
Provision a phone number via Twilio
15 min
  • Log into Twilio, buy a local number in the client's area code (~$1/mo) Match the area code to the client's city. Callers are more likely to answer callbacks from a local number.
  • In Vapi, go to Phone Numbers and connect the Twilio number to the assistant you just created
  • Note the Twilio number — this is what the client will forward their calls to
6
Connect Google Calendar booking action
30 min
  • In Vapi, add a Tool of type "Function" named checkAvailability This calls the client's Google Calendar API to check free/busy for a given date range. Use the existing service account credentials already set up for their CRM.
  • Add a second Tool named bookAppointment that creates a Calendar event with caller name, phone, and service type
  • Test both tools in the Vapi dashboard before connecting the phone number
  • Verify booked events appear in the client's calendar with correct details
Shortcut: If calendar setup is delayed, configure the agent to collect the caller's name, number, and requested time, then log it as a lead and send the client a text summary. This is a working MVP while calendar integration is finalized.
7
Set up lead logging webhook to Supabase
30 min
  • In Vapi, go to Webhooks and add an end-of-call webhook pointing to: https://[client-domain]/api/vapi-webhook — you will create this route
  • Create src/app/api/vapi-webhook/route.ts in the client's Next.js project
  • On POST, parse the Vapi payload: caller number, transcript summary, caller name (if collected), and booking status
  • Insert a row into the leads table: Fields: name (caller name or "Unknown Caller"), phone (caller number), service (mentioned service from transcript), message (transcript summary), source: "ai_receptionist"
  • Trigger the existing lead notification email (same function already used by quote forms)
  • Verify a test call appears in /pro/leads with source tag "AI Call"
Zero schema changes needed. The existing leads table already has all the fields required. The source field distinguishes AI calls from web form submissions in the CRM leads view.

Phase 3: Testing and Go-Live

8
Full end-to-end test before client touches anything
30 min
  • Call the Twilio number from your own cell. Agent should answer within 2 seconds.
  • Ask about a service and its price. Verify the answer is accurate.
  • Ask to book an appointment. Verify it lands on the calendar correctly.
  • Ask a question not in the system prompt. Verify it handles gracefully (offers callback, does not make up an answer).
  • Check /pro/leads — the test call should appear with source "AI Call" and a transcript summary.
  • Check that the lead notification email fired correctly.
  • Call from a different number and verify caller ID is captured in the lead.
9
Client walkthrough and call forwarding setup
20-30 min screen share
  • Show the client a test call in the CRM lead inbox so they see exactly what they will receive
  • Walk them through how to set up conditional call forwarding on their carrier: Most carriers: dial *61* + [Twilio number] + # to forward unanswered calls after 15-20 seconds. Instructions vary by carrier — look up AT&T / Verizon / T-Mobile specific codes.
  • Option 2: Full forward — all calls go to AI first, AI transfers to client's cell if the caller asks to speak to a person (Vapi supports call transfer natively)
  • Have the client make a live test call themselves while you watch the CRM
  • Record a short Loom walkthrough of the CRM leads view for their reference
10
Document and hand off
15 min
  • Send client a one-page summary: Twilio number, what the agent is trained on, how to read leads in the CRM
  • Add client to your internal tracker: Vapi assistant ID, Twilio number, monthly cost, go-live date
  • Set a 7-day check-in reminder to review call transcripts and tune the system prompt if needed
  • Set a 30-day reminder to review usage costs and confirm billing is correct
